摘   要  随着互联网和社交网络的快速发展 ， 网络数据的规模呈指数级增长 ， 各类复杂  网络包括但不限于 社交网络、生物网络、信息网络、交通网络、技术网络、经济  网络等 快速发展。 基于此背景，链路预测作为复杂网络分析的关键问题之一，旨  在预测网络中 当前尚未连接的但可能存在的边 ， 面临着预测精度有待提高 等问题 ，  因此常运用机器学习方法解决链路预测面临的问题。  本文主要对比了逻辑回归 （ Logistic   regre s sion ， LR ） 算法和 逻辑回归   LightGBM  堆 叠 链 路 预 测   （   Logistic-regre s sion   LightGBM   Stacking   Link   Prediction   method   ，  LLSLP ） 算法 。 其中   LR   算法是经典机器学习算法 。 LLSLP   算法 从特征提取 、 模型  构建和算法优化等方面着手。 将网络的链路预测视为一个二分类问题，考虑每两  个节点间的   8   个相似性指标，将这些相似性指标视为网络中任何两个节点间的特  征； 为了提高预测准确性和泛化性，选择差异较大的模型作为基模型。代码中采  用逻辑回归和   2   种   LightGBM   作为基模型进行集成，二者的差异有助于提升整体  性能 ， 并 采用   5   折交叉验证的方法 进行基模型的训练和预测。然后将 逻辑回归和  LightGBM   预测的链接存在与不存在的概率作为特征输入到   LightGBM   元 模型  中，实现了特征的重新利用，以提升模型的预测性能。  在五个网络上进行了对比实验，将   LLSLP   算法和   LR   算法在评估指标方面包  括   AUC 、 准确率 、 精确率 、 召回率和   F1   分数进行评估比较 ， 实验表明该算法相较  于   LR   算法具有一定的优化作用。  关键词 ： 链路预测；机器学习；逻辑回归； Stacking

Abstract  With   the   rapid   development   of   the   Internet   and   social   networks,   the   scale   of  network   data   is   growing   exponentially,   and   various   types   of   complex   networks  including   but   not   limited   to   social   networks,   biological   networks,   information   networks,  transportation   networks,   technological   networks,   economic   networks,   etc.   are  developing   rapidly.   Based   on   this   background,   link   prediction,   as   one   of   the   key  problems   in   complex   network   analysis,   aims   at   predicting   the   currently   unconnected   but  possible   edges   in   the   network   and   faces   problems   such   as   prediction   accuracy   to   be  improved,   so   machine   learning   methods   are   often   applied   to   solve   the   problems   faced  by   link   prediction.  This   dissertation   mainly   compares   the   Logistic   regres s ion   (LR)   algorithm   and   the  Logistic-regres s ion   LightGBM   Stacking   Link   Prediction   method   (LLSLP)   algorithm.  Among   them,   LR   algorithm   is   the   classical   machine   learning   algorithm.   LLSLP  algorithm   starts   from   feature   extraction,   model   construction   and   algorithm   optimization.  The   link   prediction   of   the   network   is   regarded   as   a   binary   classification   problem,   and   8  similarity   metrics   between   every   two   nodes   are   considered,   and   these   similarity   metrics  are   regarded   as   the   features   between   any   two   nodes   in   the   network;   in   order   to   improve  the   prediction   accuracy   and   generalization,   the   model   with   large   differences   is   chosen   as  the   base   model.   The   code   uses   logistic   regression   and   2   LightGBMs   as   base   models   for  integration,   and   the   differences   between   the   two   help   to   improve   the   overall  performance,   a nd   a   5-fold   cross-validation   method   was   used   for   training   and   prediction  of   the   base   model.   The   probabilities   of   the   presence   and   absence   of   links   predicted   by  logistic   regression   and   LightGBM   are   then   input   as   features   into   the   LightGBM  metamodel,   enabling   the   reuse   of   features   to   improve   the   predictive   performance   of   the  model.  Comparison   experiments   are   conducted   on   five   networks   to   evaluate   and   compare  the   LLSLP   algorithm   with   the   LR   algorithm   in   terms   of   evaluation   metrics   including  AUC,   accuracy,   precision,   recall,   and   F1   scores,   and   the   experiments   show   that   the  algorithm   is   optimized   compared   to   the   LR   algorithm.
